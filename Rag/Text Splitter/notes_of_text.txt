Text splitting is essentially chunking down the large Rag documents to create embeddings 
which can easily be accessed and processed for generating response .

we are going to see three types og splitting methods :
1. Character-Based chunking.
2. Token-Based chunking.
3. Semantic / meaning based chunking.

1. Character Based chunking :
     This is the simplest method of splitting the large documents based on the 
     the number of characters/ words or numbers.It does not account for meaning of words 
     or tokens in the document.
     
2. Token Based chunking :
     It divides the document into chunks on the basis of tokens , not the characters.
     Since LLMs work on tokens instead of words , this method aligns better with how a 
     model processes Text.

3. Semantic meaning based chunking:
     It divides a document into chunks based on the meaning and structure of the document 
     rather than the characters or tokens.
     The goal is to keep complete ideas together so that the chunks remain accessible,
     readable and meaningfull to the LLM as well as user.
     --under development.

4.📝 RecursiveTextSplitter (LangChain)
     Purpose: Splits large text into smaller chunks for LLM processing.

     Method: Uses a recursive hierarchy of separators:

     separators = ["\n\n", "\n", "  ", ""]
     Starts with \n\n (paragraphs).

     If chunks are still too large, moves to \n (lines).

     Then " " (double spaces).

     Finally "" (character-level split).

     Benefit: Ensures chunks fit within token limits while preserving context.

     Use cases: Document preprocessing for embeddings, retrieval, summarization, QA systems
